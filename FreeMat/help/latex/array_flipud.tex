\section{FLIPUD Reverse the Columns of a Matrix}

\subsection{USAGE}

Reverses the rows of a matrix.  The syntax for its use is
\begin{verbatim}
   y = flipud(x)
\end{verbatim}
where \verb|x| is matrix.  If \verb|x| is an N-dimensional array then
the first dimension is reversed.
\subsection{Example}

The following example shows \verb|flipud| applied to a 2D matrix.
\begin{verbatim}
--> x = int32(rand(4)*10)

x = 
  0  7  2  6 
  5  7 10  1 
  2  6  8  0 
  1  0  9  8 

--> flipud(x)

ans = 
  1  0  9  8 
  2  6  8  0 
  5  7 10  1 
  0  7  2  6 
\end{verbatim}
For a 3D array, note how the rows in each slice are flipped.
\begin{verbatim}
--> x = int32(rand(4,4,3)*10)

x = 

(:,:,1) = 
  8  4  6  7 
  6  0  7  6 
  8  9  9  0 
  1  2 10  5 

(:,:,2) = 
  9  6  2  8 
  3 10  1  7 
  6 10  9  8 
  3  2  4  2 

(:,:,3) = 
  3  1  5  0 
  0  6  5  1 
  9  4  9  2 
  6 10  8 10 

--> flipud(x)

ans = 

(:,:,1) = 
  1  2 10  5 
  8  9  9  0 
  6  0  7  6 
  8  4  6  7 

(:,:,2) = 
  3  2  4  2 
  6 10  9  8 
  3 10  1  7 
  9  6  2  8 

(:,:,3) = 
  6 10  8 10 
  9  4  9  2 
  0  6  5  1 
  3  1  5  0 
\end{verbatim}
